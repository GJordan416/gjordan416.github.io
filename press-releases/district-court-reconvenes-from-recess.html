PRESS RELEASE REGARDING THE DISTRICT COURT RECONVENING

The Supreme Court received and accepted a request from the Chief Judge of the District Court for the District of New Haven to relieve the lower court from indefinite leave originally requested for the Easter holiday and to suspend operations in observance of the Central Authority's abuses. The request was granted late last night.

The Court is also aware of an agreement having been reached between the Central Authority and the Chief Judge. As a result of the agreement, the District Court submitted an amendment to their local rules. The Chief Justice made the following statement:

The Central Authority is more than welcome to attempt to find an audience within the courts as a whole, rather than just the lower courts in an attempt to work out the most recent conflict that has been of a result of their own reckless decision making. The Supreme Court will not stand idle knowing that the Central Authority has attempted to give themselves unilateral power to nullify a ruling if they view it as "wrong." This would effectively render the Appellate process inadequate as members of the community could easily petition Central Authority to reverse or nullify the ruling of a case they were involved in. The Supreme Court shall also not stand idle knowing that Central Authority could easily issue a general mandate if they have an interest in any given proceeding. This attempt that Central Authority has made to make themselves an overseer of the Judicial Branch is a direct threat to the independence and functionality of the Judicial Branch as a whole.

The Court adopted a motion to reject the District Court's amendment to local rule and the agreement made with the Central Authority in-full.
